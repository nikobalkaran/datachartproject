Republicans are Steadily Gaining Ground as More New Yorkers Vote Red

By Niko Balkaran

The 2024 Presidential Election is over, and Donald Trump has officially defeated Kamala Harris.

There was a noticeable red wave that swept the country even in historically blue states like New York City.

Kendrick Holmes, a senior lead of financial services, said he was inspired to register as a Republican because of their stance on policies.

“At a federal level, these policies have a big impact on the people I love the most,” said Holmes, a 30-year-old Hong Kong immigrant.

In every election cycle since 2016, the Republican Party has gained votes in every New York City borough. The party saw the highest rise in the Bronx, with an almost 63% increase from 2016 to 2024. While Staten Island had the lowest increase of about 18%, it is the only district the Republicans won in the last three election cycles. Brooklyn, Manhattan, and Queens all fell in the 30% median.
